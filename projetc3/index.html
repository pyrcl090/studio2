Recording–Transmission–Playback

recording +

In the initial recording of this project, I used simple devices:

    [analog]
      pencil
      paper

    [digital]
      phone camera

transmission +

As I projected my recording the initial recording of my project was distorted in two ways:

    [meaning]
      the object's meaning and significance was distorted as the presentation displayed it as a set of data points to be taken account of.
      paper

    [size]
      the pysical size of the object which was a small drawing became extremely large once projected.

playback +

During the playback, I drew from the ideas of the size distortion, and loss of meaning, and created an extremely small digital receipt in the form of a noodle, creating a different perception of the initial observation.

    The goal for my playback is for me to loose control of the recording in the same way I do digitally.

    For this to occur I ask that input be created from my peers. The input can be an observation from my presentation, your mood, another piece of something, anything pertaining to the situation.

    In relinquishing control there is a mechanism [all those that participate and the devices they use] that input information to further distorting, and creating a new.

control and distortion +

In making my project editable to others while still maintaining the devices used and the use of parameters. I am showing the same process that my original project had gone through, and also completely distorting the original idea and image into something new.
